# PRODUCT

## Product Name
flyscan

## Register
product

## Product Purpose
flyscan is a flight search and comparison interface for evaluating itinerary options across providers. It is built for quickly scanning tradeoffs between price, duration, layover time, and stops, then drilling into itinerary details before booking.

The product should make messy travel-search results feel structured, comparable, and trustworthy.

## Users
- Price-conscious travelers comparing tradeoffs across multiple itineraries
- Travel planners who care about total trip quality, not just lowest fare
- Users reviewing results on both desktop and mobile, often while multitasking

## User Context
Users are typically in evaluation mode, not browsing casually. They want to answer questions like:
- What is the best-value trip?
- How much extra am I paying to save time or avoid stops?
- Which itineraries are acceptable once I tighten filters?

They are often under mild time pressure and may be comparing several options at once.

## Emotional Goals
The interface should evoke:
- confidence
- clarity
- control
- momentum

It should avoid making flight shopping feel chaotic, cheap, or gimmicky.

## Brand Personality
Three words:
- precise
- practical
- composed

Tone should feel like a sharp comparison tool, not a glossy booking funnel.

## Visual Direction
- Dark mode first
- Dense but readable
- Strong numeric hierarchy
- Calm, controlled motion
- Utility-led rather than decorative

## References
Useful reference qualities:
- Financial/productivity tools with strong information hierarchy
- Airline/travel products that surface tradeoffs clearly
- Interfaces that reward scanning and comparison rather than storytelling

## Anti-References
This should not feel like:
- a generic consumer travel affiliate site
- a bright, cluttered booking funnel
- a playful consumer lifestyle brand
- a dashboard overloaded with cards, badges, and decorative chrome

## Design Principles
- Prioritize comparison over decoration.
- Make the current best action and current constraints legible at a glance.
- Numeric information should drive hierarchy.
- Motion should reinforce state changes, never distract from evaluation.
- Mobile layouts should preserve decision quality, not just stack desktop sections.
